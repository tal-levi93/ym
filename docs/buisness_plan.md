## 1. Business Overview

### What You Do

You build custom technology solutions for small/medium businesses who want to modernize their operations but can't afford (or don't need) full custom enterprise software.

**Core Services:**
- Custom React websites (professional, fast, modern)

## 8. Common Challenges & Solutions

### Issues You'll Face and How to Handle Them

---

### Challenge 1: "That's too expensive"

**Solution:**
- Break down the value (not features)
- Show ROI calculation
- Compare to cost of employee doing it manually
- Offer payment plan (50% down, 25% at midpoint, 25% at completion)
- Start with smaller package

**Script:**
*"I understand the investment feels significant. Let me break down what you're getting: This automation alone will save your team 15 hours per week. That's 780 hours per year. At $30/hour, that's $23,400 in saved labor costs annually. The $14,000 investment pays for itself in about 7 months."*

---

### Challenge 2: "Can't you just use WordPress/Wix?"

**Solution:**
- Explain the difference
- Show performance comparison
- Highlight ownership and customization

**Script:**
*"I could, but here's why I don't: WordPress sites are slower, harder to maintain, and you're locked into that platform. With a custom React site, you own the code, it's 3x faster, more secure, and I can build any custom feature you need. Template builders limit what's possible. I build what you actually need."*

---

### Challenge 3: "I can't afford the monthly retainer"

**Solution:**
- Explain what happens without support
- Show cost of hiring developer hourly
- Offer time banking option
- Highlight proactive monitoring value

**Script:**
*"The retainer ensures your systems stay running smoothly. Without it, when something breaks, you're stuck. The average hourly rate for a freelance developer is $100-150/hour. My $750 retainer includes 6 hours - that's $125/hour, plus proactive monitoring. When issues arise, I catch them before they impact your business."*

**Alternative:**
- Offer "break-fix" model at $150/hour (no retainer)
- Most clients come back to retainer after first emergency

---

### Challenge 4: Scope Creep

**Problem:** Client keeps asking for "one more small thing"

**Solution:**
- Clear contracts with scope definition
- Change request process
- Document everything
- Regular check-ins to manage expectations

**Process:**
1. Client requests change
2. You evaluate: "Is this in scope?"
3. If yes: Include in current sprint
4. If no: "This is outside our original scope. Here are options:"
   - Add to next month's retainer hours
   - One-time fee of $X
   - Include in next phase

**Script:**
*"That's a great idea! This would be a change to the original scope we agreed on. I can definitely build it - it would take about [X] hours at $150/hour, or we can roll it into next month's retainer if it's not urgent. What works better for you?"*

---

### Challenge 5: Client Doesn't Pay

**Prevention:**
- 50% deposit before starting
- 25% at midpoint
- 25% before final delivery
- Never deploy to production until fully paid
- Auto-billing for retainers (Stripe subscriptions)

**If it happens:**
1. Friendly reminder (maybe they forgot)
2. Formal notice after 7 days
3. Pause work after 14 days
4. Suspend services after 30 days
5. Collections or legal after 60 days

**In Contract:**
- Late fees (1.5% per month)
- Right to suspend services
- Client pays collection costs

---

### Challenge 6: Technical Issue You Can't Solve

**Solution:**
- Network with other developers
- Use AI assistants (Claude, ChatGPT)
- Stack Overflow / Dev communities
- Hire specialist consultant if needed (build cost into quote)

**Be Honest with Client:**
*"This is outside my expertise. I can research and figure it out (estimate X hours), or I can bring in a specialist I trust (estimate Y cost). What's your preference?"*

Clients respect honesty more than fake confidence.

---

### Challenge 7: Choosing Between Clients/Projects

**Early on, take most projects to:**
- Build portfolio
- Get testimonials
- Generate cash flow
- Learn

**As you grow, be selective:**
- Clients who don't respect your time = fire them
- Clients who don't pay on time = fire them
- Projects outside your expertise = pass to others
- Red flags in discovery call = walk away

**Red Flags:**
- "My last 3 developers quit"
- "This should be quick and easy" (it never is)
- Constantly changing mind
- Wants you to build something illegal/unethical
- Unwilling to pay deposit
- Expects 24/7 availability

## 15. Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Learn from Others' Failures

---

### Mistake 1: Underpricing

**Problem:**
- Charge too little to "compete"
- Work 80 hours for $3,000
- Attract worst clients
- Can't sustain business

**Solution:**
- Price for the value delivered, not hours
- If project saves client $50K/year, $15K is cheap
- Start higher, negotiate down if needed (never opposite)
- Fire cheap clients when you can

---

### Mistake 2: No Contracts

**Problem:**
- "They're a friend, don't need contract"
- Scope creep destroys profit
- Disputes over payment
- No legal protection

**Solution:**
- ALWAYS use contracts
- Even with friends (especially with friends!)
- Clearly define scope
- Payment terms in writing
- Get signatures before starting

---

### Mistake 3: Not Tracking Time

**Problem:**
- Don't know if profitable
- Can't price future projects accurately
- Retainer hours get abused

**Solution:**
- Track every hour with Toggl or similar
- Review monthly: "Am I profitable per client?"
- Adjust pricing based on reality
- Fire unprofitable clients

---

### Mistake 4: Doing Everything Client Asks

**Problem:**
- Scope creep kills projects
- Work for free
- Projects never end
- Resentment builds

**Solution:**
- Everything beyond original scope = change request
- Document change, get approval, charge accordingly
- Learn to say: "Great idea! That would be $X extra"
- Clients respect boundaries

---

### Mistake 5: Not Having Emergency Fund

**Problem:**
- Client doesn't pay
- No projects for 2 months
- Computer breaks
- Can't pay rent = panic = bad decisions

**Solution:**
- Save 6 months of expenses ASAP
- Keep this sacred (don't touch)
- Allows you to be selective with clients
- Allows you to fire bad clients
- Peace of mind

**Goal:** ‚Ç™60,000-100,000 emergency fund

---

### Mistake 6: Trying to Serve Everyone

**Problem:**
- "I can build anything for anyone!"
- No clear positioning
- Hard to market
- Mediocre at everything

**Solution:**
- Pick a niche (at least initially)
- "I build automation solutions for Israeli law firms"
- "I create websites for health clinics"
- Easier to market
- Command higher prices
- Build specific expertise

**You can expand later**
